Section 08

60-Day
Roadmap

Week-by-week execution plan from pre-launch through to Month 2 scale. Weekly client calls throughout the first 60 days.

Pre-Launch Week 0
Foundation — Before a Dollar Is Spent
  • Full Pixel audit via Meta Pixel Helper — verify all 4 standard events fire correctly
  • Confirm Conversions API is live for server-side tracking redundancy
  • Finalise and upload all 7–10 launch creatives with UTM parameters
  • Build all 3 campaigns in Ads Manager — do NOT activate MOF/BOF yet
  • Client alignment call: budget sign-off, creative approvals, LP decision
Week 1
Launch — TOF Goes Live
  • Launch TOF Prospecting only — 2 ad sets, all creatives running, $67/day total
  • Monitor for ad approvals, policy flags, and Pixel event quality daily
  • Day 5–7: Activate Retargeting campaign once ViewContent hits 200+ events
  • First weekly call — review early CTR and creative signals
Week 2
First Optimisation Pass
  • Pause any ad with CTR below 0.8% after 1,000+ impressions
  • Double budget allocation toward creatives with CTR above 2.0%
  • Brief Round 2 creatives based on Week 1 winning angles
  • Activate BOF ad set within Retargeting if ATC events exceed 20+
Weeks 3–4
Learning Phase Completion
  • Test shifting TOF objective to ATC conversion if 50+ events recorded
  • Round 2 creatives enter rotation — maintain 5+ active ads per ad set
  • Monitor frequency closely — introduce new creative if any ad set hits 3.0+
  • End-of-Month 1 full report delivered to client before call
Weeks 5–6
Scale Winners
  • Identify top 2 creatives. Increase budget allocation by 20%
  • Build LAL 1% audience once custom audience reaches 1,000+ users
  • Begin planning cabinet-focused creative set for Month 2 introduction
  • Landing page review — if site CVR below 0.8%, escalate LP creation
Weeks 7–8
Month 2 Expansion
  • Launch cabinet ad set within TOF campaign (10–15% of TOF budget)
  • Full funnel attribution review in GA4 — confirm Meta-to-purchase path
  • Month 2 report. ROAS target: 1.5–2.0x. Propose budget increase if hitting target
  • Test 2% and 3% LAL audiences for volume expansion

Section 10

Risk
Mitigation

Identified risks with probability ratings and specific mitigation strategies for each.

Medium Risk

Creative Underperformance

Ads don't achieve 1.5% CTR, restricting algorithm learning and wasting budget during critical learning phase.

Mitigation

Launch 7–10 diverse creatives. Kill non-performers fast. Brief new creatives every 2 weeks. Creative quality is the #1 risk factor at this budget level.

Low Risk

Ad Account Policy Flags

Meta ad account rejection or policy violations that disrupt campaign continuity and waste setup time.

Mitigation

Pre-approve all creatives through internal review before launch. Avoid superlative claims. No before/after imagery that implies unrealistic results.

Medium Risk

Insufficient Pixel Data

Delays shift from Traffic to Conversion optimisation if 50+ ATC events aren't reached in time.

Mitigation

Run Traffic campaigns in Month 1 to feed the Pixel. Do not rush to conversion optimisation before 50+ ATC events.

Low Risk

iOS Attribution Gaps

iOS signal loss potentially undercounting Meta conversions and making ROAS appear lower than actual.

Mitigation

Implement CAPI (Conversions API) — eliminates 60–70% of iOS signal loss. Confirm CAPI is live pre-launch.

Medium Risk

Budget Limits Testing Velocity

$2,000/month slows the learning cycle compared to a larger budget, extending time to optimisation.

Mitigation

Prioritise creative diversity over audience diversity. With $2,000/month Meta budget, creative is the lever — not more audiences.

Low Risk

Competitor Enters Meta

A competitor launches Meta ads and increases CPM competition in the home improvement category.

Mitigation

Speed and creative quality are the moat. First-mover advantage compounds. Build audience data now that competitors cannot replicate.
